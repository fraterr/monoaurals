What is a Real Monaural Beat?

Unlike binaural beats (which require headphones because they send different frequencies to each ear, letting the brain integrate them), monaural beats are physically mixed directly in the audio signal itself.

Two sound waves with slightly different frequencies physically interfere with each other, creating a physical amplitude (volume) pulsation that stimulates the auditory system. This acoustic phenomenon works perfectly with both headphones and standard speakers, making it ideal for reproduction in your bedroom or office via external speakers.
